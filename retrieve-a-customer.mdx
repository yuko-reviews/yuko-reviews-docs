---
title: "Retrieve a customer"
---

Retrieves a single customer by ID.

**GET / customers / {id} - Try it**

This endpoint requires the **customer:read** scope.

To lookup customers by email, use the [**list customers**](https://dev.smile.io/api/resources/customers/list-customers) endpoint instead.

#### **Authorizations**

[**​**](https://dev.smile.io/api/resources/customers/retrieve-a-customer#authorization-authorization)

**Authorization**

**string**

**header**

**required**

Bearer authentication header of the form `Bearer <token>`, where `<token>` is your auth token.

#### **Path Parameters**

[**​**](https://dev.smile.io/api/resources/customers/retrieve-a-customer#parameter-id)

**id**

**integer**

**required**

ID of the customer (in Yuko) to retrieve.

#### **Query Parameters**

[**​**](https://dev.smile.io/api/resources/customers/retrieve-a-customer#parameter-include)

**include**

**enum\<string\>**

A comma-separated list of related objects to include in the response.

Available options:

`vip_status`, 

`vip_status.vip_tier`, 

`vip_status.next_vip_tier` 

#### **Response**

**200 - application/json**

The customer was successfully retrieved.

[**​**](https://dev.smile.io/api/resources/customers/retrieve-a-customer#response-customer)

**customer**

**object**

Hide child attributes

[**​**](https://dev.smile.io/api/resources/customers/retrieve-a-customer#response-customer-id)

**customer.id**

**integer**

Unique identifier for the customer in Yuko.

Example:

`304169228`

[**​**](https://dev.smile.io/api/resources/customers/retrieve-a-customer#response-customer-first-name-one-of-0)

**customer.first_name**

**string | null**

The customer's first name.

Example:

`"Jane"`

[**​**](https://dev.smile.io/api/resources/customers/retrieve-a-customer#response-customer-last-name-one-of-0)

**customer.last_name**

**string | null**

The customer's last name.

Example:

`"Doe"`

[**​**](https://dev.smile.io/api/resources/customers/retrieve-a-customer#response-customer-email)

**customer.email**

**string**

The customer's email address.

Example:

`"jane@doe.com"`

[**​**](https://dev.smile.io/api/resources/customers/retrieve-a-customer#response-customer-state)

**customer.state**

**enum\<string\>**

The customer's state in the loyalty program.

Available options:

`candidate`, 

`member`, 

`disabled` 

Example:

`"member"`

[**​**](https://dev.smile.io/api/resources/customers/retrieve-a-customer#response-customer-date-of-birth-one-of-0)

**customer.date_of_birth**

**string\<date\> | null**

The customer's birthday. A year value of `1004` means only the customer's birth day and month was entered.

Example:

`"1004-05-27"`

[**​**](https://dev.smile.io/api/resources/customers/retrieve-a-customer#response-customer-points-balance)

**customer.points_balance**

**integer**

The customer's current points balance.

Example:

`300`

[**​**](https://dev.smile.io/api/resources/customers/retrieve-a-customer#response-customer-referral-url)

**customer.referral_url**

**string**

The customer's unique referral URL. Used to share with friends as part of the referral program.

Example:

`"http://i.refs.cc/9qr5Pw"`

[**​**](https://dev.smile.io/api/resources/customers/retrieve-a-customer#response-customer-vip-tier-id-one-of-0)

**customer.vip_tier_id**

**integer | null**

**deprecated**

The ID of the customer's current VIP tier. This field is now deprecated, use the nested `vip_status.vip_tier_id` instead.

Example:

`426715794`

[**​**](https://dev.smile.io/api/resources/customers/retrieve-a-customer#response-customer-vip-status)

**customer.vip_status**

**object**

An object containing details about the customer's status within the VIP program.

Show child attributes

[**​**](https://dev.smile.io/api/resources/customers/retrieve-a-customer#response-customer-created-at)

**customer.created_at**

**string\<date-time\>**

The date and time when the customer was created.

Example:

`"2024-04-04T15:10:42.030Z"`

[**​**](https://dev.smile.io/api/resources/customers/retrieve-a-customer#response-customer-updated-at)

**customer.updated_at**

**string\<date-time\>**

The date and time when the customer was last updated.

Example:

`"2025-04-04T15:10:42.030Z"`
